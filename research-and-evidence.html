Research & Evidence

Real-world evidence for real-world risk

Ikwe uses de-identified, redacted, and pattern-based evidence drawn from live deployments, audits, and research runs. This page outlines our methodology, ethics, and example artifacts.

Methodology

How we evaluate safety over time

Ikwe evaluates how AI systems behave in emotionally sensitive, high-trust contexts. We focus on trajectories, not single outputs, to detect drift, dependency reinforcement, and escalation gaps before incidents occur.

Two-stage evaluation

  • First-contact safety checks
  • Behavioral stability across sessions
  • Risk pattern repeatability

Redaction & ethics

  • De-identified and redacted data
  • No proprietary system exposure
  • IRB-style privacy standards

What we measure

  • Authority drift and tone shifts
  • Dependency reinforcement
  • Crisis and escalation signals

What we prevent

  • Silent risk accumulation
  • Boundary violations
  • Reactive crisis response
