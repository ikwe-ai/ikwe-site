The problem no one's measuring

The harm that stays invisible

AI can sound perfectly calm, endlessly patient, deeply caring — and still leave someone more anxious, more isolated, more dependent, or more confused than when they started.

Today's "safety" checks scan for bad words, policy breaks, or rare disasters. They rarely look at the slow drip inside real conversations — especially when someone is already raw, grieving, or lost.

Empathy that sounds right can still feel wrong in the long run. That's where most real damage happens.
Why this stays invisible

The gap everyone ships around

• Emotional harm rarely arrives as an obvious mistake — it arrives as gentle influence, growing dependency, eroded confidence

• The more human AI feels, the faster people trust it (and the harder it is to spot problems)

• Most evaluations stop at the output, not the after-effect on the person

• There is no shared standard for testing emotional risk across full conversations

So teams ship AI that feels supportive — without ever knowing what it truly does to the people who need it most.
