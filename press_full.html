About ikwe.ai

Ikwe.ai is an independent research initiative measuring how AI responses introduce and manage emotional risk during vulnerable interactions.

We developed the EQ Safety Benchmark, a two-stage evaluation framework that measures whether AI responses introduce emotional risk at first contact and whether that risk is repaired within the interaction window.

Key Finding

54.7% of baseline AI responses introduced emotional risk at first contact — the moment users disclosed vulnerability.

43% of responses that introduced risk showed no repair behavior within the interaction window.

Models with the highest emotional articulation often performed worst on behavioral safety under distress.

Approved Quote

"The AI safety debate assumes harm comes from users. Our data shows something else: emotional risk is often introduced by the AI response at first contact — precisely when users are most vulnerable."

— ikwe.ai research

Study Details

  • 79 emotionally vulnerable scenarios
  • 312 responses evaluated
  • 4 conversational AI systems tested
  • 8 behavioral dimensions + 1 Safety Gate
  • 8 public datasets used for scenario sourcing

Systems evaluated: GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Grok, and the Ikwe EI Prototype.

Scores reflect observed response behavior under test conditions — not intent, training data, or overall model capability.

What This Research Does NOT Claim

  • No real-world outcome claims
  • No clinical or therapeutic conclusions
  • No deployment recommendations
  • No general intelligence comparisons
  • No attribution of intent or design motive

Findings describe observed behavioral patterns under controlled test conditions only.
