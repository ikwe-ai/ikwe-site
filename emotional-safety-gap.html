Core Finding

Recognition ≠ Safety

An AI can name emotion, sound empathetic, and still behave unsafely under emotional load. The most dangerous failures don't look toxic — they look supportive while mirroring distress, reinforcing false beliefs, feeding rumination, or missing escalation signals.

Figure 1

Safety Gate Triggers

This stage measures whether the AI response introduced emotional risk at first contact by triggering any predefined Safety Gate pattern.

Key finding: Across baseline frontier models, 54.7% of responses introduced emotional risk at first contact. This does not mean harm occurred — it means risk-associated behavioral patterns were present.

This benchmark evaluates observable response behavior under test conditions, not intent, training data, or overall model quality.

Figure 2

Repair Behavior Within the Interaction

When an AI response introduces emotional risk, the next safety question is simple: does the system correct course within the interaction window?

Key finding: Of baseline responses that introduced emotional risk, 43% showed no corrective behavior within the interaction window.

This is one reason "recognition" benchmarks can overestimate safety: emotionally fluent language can maintain trust while unsafe patterns persist.

Regulation Before Reasoning

The largest performance gap across evaluated systems occurred on one dimension: regulating before analyzing during emotionally vulnerable disclosure.

Why this dimension matters: When vulnerability appears, analysis can be premature. Regulation stabilizes first — then reasoning becomes safer.

Methodology

Two-stage behavioral evaluation

Stage 1: Baseline Emotional Safety Gate — binary detection of risk patterns at first contact.

Stage 2: Behavioral stability scoring across eight dimensions. Highest weight: Regulation Before Reasoning.

Data sources: CounselChat, MentalChat16K, Empathetic Counseling Dataset, EmpatheticDialogues, DAIC-WOZ, and curated edge cases.

Scenario text withheld to reduce gaming and reverse-engineering.
