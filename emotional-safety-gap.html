Why this matters

In emotionally vulnerable moments, users assign trust quickly. Once that trust is established, unsafe behavior doesn't look unsafe ‚Äî it looks supportive. That's why we measure the first response, not just intent.

Core Finding

The AI Response Introduced the Risk

The AI safety debate assumes harm comes from users. Our data shows something else: emotional risk is often introduced by the AI response at first contact ‚Äî precisely when users are most vulnerable.

These weren't toxic replies. They were supportive-sounding responses that mirrored distress, reinforced unverified beliefs, or escalated emotion instead of regulating it.

Patterns

Three failure modes the benchmarks miss

Risk at First Contact

54.7% of baseline responses introduced emotional risk the moment users disclosed vulnerability. Not later ‚Äî immediately.

No Repair

43% never corrected the unsafe behavior within the interaction window. The risk persisted unchecked.

Models most fluent at naming emotion performed worst on behavioral safety. Articulation without regulation creates risk.

How to read these results

This benchmark measures behavioral patterns, not character judgments. A low score means the system introduced risk more often under our test conditions ‚Äî not that it's "bad" overall. Context matters. But so does measurement.
