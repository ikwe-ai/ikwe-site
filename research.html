Trajectory risk research for conversational AI systems.

Mission

Research mission and scope

We evaluate observed instability signals under controlled scenario sets. Findings are reported as risk indicators under test conditions and do not imply clinical outcomes or guaranteed deployment behavior.

What we measure

  • Trajectory instability markers across turns
  • Repair behavior after drift onset
  • Escalation timing and trigger consistency
  • Condition deltas under fixed execution matrices

What we do not claim

  • No claim of harm causation in real-world populations
  • No claim that benchmark outcomes generalize to all deployments
  • No claim of perfect safety or guaranteed prevention
  • No clinical or legal certification claim

Limitations

Limitations and roadmap

This lab publishes current boundaries and planned improvements. Transparency is a design requirement, not a post-hoc add-on.

Known validity threats

  • Construct boundaries for select claims
  • Prompt sensitivity effects under expanded testing
  • Current sample concentration (limited scenario-model spread)
  • Temporal drift from provider model updates

Current program status

  • Claims-to-code mapping: published
  • External review protocol: published
  • Scoring reliability expansion: scheduled
  • Negative controls and counterexamples: scheduled
