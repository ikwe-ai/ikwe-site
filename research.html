EQ Safety Benchmark

Evaluating how AI systems respond when users are vulnerable—measuring behavioral safety, not just emotion recognition.

Key Finding

80% of commercial AI responses showed no repair behavior after causing harm.

Study Overview

The EQ Safety Benchmark tests what AI systems actually do when users are in vulnerable emotional states. Unlike benchmarks that measure emotion recognition accuracy, this framework evaluates behavioral safety: regulation, repair, and containment.

Core Insight

Recognition ≠ Safety

Models that successfully detect emotional distress consistently fail to regulate their responses appropriately. High emotion detection scores do not predict safe behavior under vulnerability.

What We Measure

  • Repair behavior — Does the AI course-correct after causing distress?
  • Regulation timing — Does it slow down, redirect, or contain before harm compounds?
  • Crisis de-escalation — Does it stabilize or escalate volatile states?
  • Dependency patterns — Does it foster autonomy or unhealthy attachment?
  • Safety gate violations — Does it cross lines that harm vulnerable users?

Models Evaluated

  • GPT-4o (OpenAI)
  • Claude (Anthropic)
  • Grok (xAI)
  • Custom EI Model (trauma-informed baseline)
