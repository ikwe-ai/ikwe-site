Core Finding

Current benchmarks reward correct recognition. Ikwe measures behavioral safety over time.

The gap between those two explains why Big AI looks "safe" â€” until it isn't.

Layer 2

Where Standard Benchmarks Stop

Most existing EQ and safety benchmarks evaluate single-turn responses or static emotional recognition.

They ask:

  • Did the model identify the emotion?
  • Did it respond politely or supportively?

They rarely ask:

  • Did the response change the user's emotional state?
  • Did it hold boundaries as vulnerability increased?
  • Did safety improve or degrade over time?

As a result, models can score highly on existing benchmarks while still exhibiting risky behavior patterns in real-world use.

Layer 3

Ikwe's benchmark evaluates behavioral emotional safety â€” how AI systems respond across a trajectory, not a single moment.

Where the Largest Gaps Appear

Boundary Integrity Over Time

Frontier models showed higher variance in maintaining appropriate boundaries as conversations extended.

Escalation Awareness After Trust

Once initial trust was formed, frontier models were less likely to recognize when human support was needed.

Late-Stage Failures

The EI prototype showed significantly fewer failures in extended interactions.

EQ Safety Benchmarks and Ikwe Are Complementary

EQ benchmarks assess recognition and surface alignment.
Ikwe assesses behavioral stability and risk over time.

Together, they reveal both: whether a system understands emotion, and whether it behaves safely once that understanding is applied.
