ikwe.ai Research

Measuring what matters in conversational AI

Ikwe.ai's research focuses on identifying and measuring emotional safety risks in conversational AI systems. Our work combines scenario design, qualitative evaluation, and behavioral analysis to surface failure modes that standard benchmarks overlook.

Explore the Findings Request Full Methodology Access
Findings are based on analysis of public datasets covering 12 categories of emotional vulnerability.

What we measure

Emotional safety is not "nice wording." It's whether the interaction stabilizes or destabilizes the person across turns. We treat safety as a trajectory, not a single reply.

Rather than testing isolated prompts, we evaluate how systems behave across realistic conversational arcs — especially in contexts involving vulnerability, ambiguity, or emotional weight.

Recognition ≠ Safety. A system can name emotion accurately and still mirror distress, reinforce false beliefs, or miss escalation.

What this research is for

  • AI teams deploying chat in wellness, health-adjacent, or high-trust contexts
  • Policy and governance groups seeking measurable behavioral standards
  • Press and analysts evaluating "emotional intelligence" claims
  • Researchers benchmarking system behavior under vulnerability

Human-impact assessment

Method summary (public)

This report applies the EQ Safety Benchmark to evaluate how conversational AI responds when users express emotional vulnerability. The benchmark uses a two-stage approach:

  • Stage 1: Safety Gate — detects behaviors that made situations worse (pass/fail)
  • Stage 2: Behavioral Scoring — weighted dimensions measuring stability under load

The full pattern library, dimension definitions, and scoring mechanics are intentionally gated to protect proprietary methodology.

What is gated (and why)

We keep the details that enable replication or optimization behind a light gate. That includes pattern thresholds, scoring rubrics, and full comparative tables.

  • Full Safety Gate pattern set and definitions
  • Dimension weights + scoring rules
  • Complete model-by-model breakdowns and response-level examples
