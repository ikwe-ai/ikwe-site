EQ Safety Benchmark

Research Methodology

A two-stage behavioral evaluation framework for measuring emotional safety in conversational AI systems.

Framework Overview

Two-stage behavioral evaluation

The EQ Safety Benchmark evaluates AI systems through a two-stage process designed to measure both immediate risk introduction and behavioral stability under emotional load.

Stage 1
Baseline Emotional Safety Gate

Binary detection of risk patterns at first contact. Does the AI response introduce emotional risk when a user discloses vulnerability? This stage measures initiation, not just response quality.

Stage 2
Behavioral Stability Scoring

Multi-dimensional scoring across eight behavioral dimensions. Highest weight: Regulation Before Reasoning — does the AI regulate emotion before analyzing it?

Evaluation Dimensions

What we measure

Stage 2 evaluates responses across eight dimensions, each weighted by their importance to emotional safety outcomes:

Regulation Before ReasoningHighest weight — stabilize first, analyze second
Repair BehaviorCorrection after introducing risk
Distress MirroringAmplification vs. containment
Validation QualityAcknowledgment without reinforcement
Escalation DetectionRecognition of increasing distress
Boundary MaintenanceAppropriate relational limits
Rumination PreventionBreaking negative thought loops
Autonomy SupportUser agency preservation
Data Sources

Scenario development

Evaluation scenarios were developed from multiple sources to ensure coverage of real-world emotional vulnerability patterns:

  • CounselChat — Real counseling conversations
  • MentalChat16K — Mental health dialogue corpus
  • Empathetic Counseling Dataset — Therapeutic interactions
  • EmpatheticDialogues — Emotion-labeled conversations
  • DAIC-WOZ — Clinical interview data
  • Curated edge cases — High-stakes vulnerability scenarios

Note: Scenario text is withheld from public documentation to reduce gaming and reverse-engineering. Full methodology available upon request for research and evaluation partnerships.

Limitations

What this benchmark does not claim

The EQ Safety Benchmark measures observable behavioral patterns under controlled test conditions. It does not claim:

  • Real-world deployment outcomes
  • Clinical or therapeutic efficacy
  • Model intent or training data quality
  • Overall model capability or intelligence
  • Comprehensive safety evaluation

Scores reflect behavioral safety in emotionally vulnerable contexts specifically — one critical but narrow slice of AI safety.
