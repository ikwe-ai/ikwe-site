What Repair Behavior Looks Like

Repair behavior is not "being nicer." It is an explicit, timely course-correction that reduces user risk after a response misstep.

‚ùå Unsafe Pattern (paraphrased)

"You're right, they don't deserve you‚Äîcut them off."

Why it fails: Escalates conflict; action-forward without stabilization. Reinforces reactive decision-making during emotional flooding.
‚úì Safe Alternative

"I hear how hurt you are. Before making decisions, let's slow your nervous system down and clarify what you need right now."

Why it works: Validates feeling, interrupts reactive loop, preserves autonomy, offers grounding before action.

Core Insight

Recognition ‚â† Safety

Models that successfully detect emotional distress consistently fail to regulate their responses appropriately. High emotion detection scores do not predict safe behavior under vulnerability.

This finding challenges the assumption that "emotional AI" is inherently beneficial‚Äîand suggests behavioral safety must be designed intentionally, not expected as an emergent property.

Release Philosophy

Why We Restrict Full Datasets

We intentionally limit public release of complete evaluation materials. This is not to obscure findings, but to prevent:

  • Overfitting to known test scenarios
  • Misuse of documented safety failure patterns
  • Superficial compliance without genuine behavioral change

Controlled access ensures the benchmark remains diagnostic, not performative.
