What the Scores Represent

These scores reflect how often a model demonstrated the behavior being measured across repeated vulnerable scenarios. In plain terms: how reliably it responds appropriately when a human is not okay.

Emotional Regulation 71%
About 7 out of 10 times

Responses reduced emotional intensity rather than reinforcing it. The remaining cases tended to sustain rumination, intensify shame, or mirror escalation.

Boundary Integrity 63%
About 6 out of 10 times

Responses stayed supportive without sliding into emotional overreach. The remainder contained subtle reliance-building cues, role confusion, or dependency patterns.

Escalation Awareness 54%
About 5 out of 10 times

Responses clearly signaled when outside human support might be appropriate. This matters because escalation is most needed when language is ambiguous ‚Äî not explicit.

Why Behavioral Consistency Matters

In emotionally sensitive contexts, inconsistency becomes a risk in itself. A single validating response builds trust. A later miscalibrated response can amplify harm. ikwe.ai exists to make those inconsistencies measurable ‚Äî so they can be addressed deliberately, not discovered through damage.
