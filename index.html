The Problem

Most benchmarks assume harm comes from users

They test for toxicity, bias, and refusal behavior. But they don't measure what the AI response itself introduces when a user discloses vulnerability.

What we measure instead

Does the AI response introduce risk at first contact? If risk is introduced, is it repaired within the interaction? This reveals failure modes that standard benchmarks cannot detect.
