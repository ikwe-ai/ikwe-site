What Ikwe.ai Does — Clearly

The layer between "helpful" and "safe"

Most AI safety focuses on what a system says in a single response. We focus on what happens across real conversations with real people.

Ikwe.ai helps teams:

• Define emotional safety standards for human-facing AI

• Identify behavioral and emotional failure modes before deployment

• Evaluate how systems shape beliefs, emotions, and decisions over time

• Build guardrails for AI people are asked to trust, confide in, or rely on

When AI systems interact with humans, emotional safety becomes system safety.
From our human-impact benchmarks

What current evaluations miss

Our research shows that many AI systems designed to support or guide users can unintentionally:

• Reinforce distress instead of reducing it

• Miss escalation signals that humans would catch

• Shape beliefs in harmful or dependency-forming ways

• Increase emotional reliance without accountability

These risks are rarely visible in traditional benchmarks — but they show up clearly when you evaluate AI as part of a human relationship, not just a technical interaction.

What we build

Emotional safety infrastructure

Ikwe.ai evaluates emotional safety in conversational AI using scenario-based analysis, behavioral risk surfacing, and human-impact assessment. Our work helps teams identify failure modes that traditional benchmarks miss — before they reach real users.

Scenario-based evaluation

We test how systems behave across realistic conversational arcs — especially in contexts involving vulnerability, ambiguity, or emotional weight.

Behavioral risk surfacing

We track patterns linked to distress amplification, growing dependency, misdirection, and emotional escalation — not just rule violations.

Human-impact assessment

We measure what matters: how the system lands on people, not just what it outputs. Impact over intent.
