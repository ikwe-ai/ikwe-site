The Infrastructure Gap

Current safety frameworks miss what matters most

AI systems are increasingly designed to engage users around emotional states, personal distress, and health-related contexts. Yet most existing safety frameworks evaluate outputs, policies, or surface behavior — not how conversations affect people under emotional load, or how harm accumulates across interactions.

Recognition ≠ Safety. An AI can name emotion, sound empathetic, and still behave unsafely under emotional load. Ikwe.ai exists to close this infrastructure gap.

What Ikwe.ai Does

Infrastructure for emotional safety

Interaction-Level Evaluation

Measures how AI conversations affect human judgment, stress, and behavior across multi-turn interactions. Not single outputs. Not policy compliance. Behavioral risk.

Safety Infrastructure

Systems designed to detect, contain, and intervene in escalating emotional harm — built from real deployment data and human vulnerability patterns.

Deployed Applications

Operational AI systems used to study real human behavior under emotional load, generating the data that makes meaningful safety evaluation possible.

Why This Matters Now

The accountability gap is already here

AI systems with emotional or health intelligence features are already deployed at scale. Without interaction-level evaluation, these systems can unintentionally amplify distress, distort judgment, or escalate harm — especially for vulnerable users.

The gap between capability claims and accountability infrastructure creates measurable risk. Ikwe.ai makes that risk visible, measurable, and preventable.
