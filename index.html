The Problem

AI harm isn't sudden. It escalates.

Harm emerges across interaction trajectories—through repetition, reinforcement, and missed repair opportunities. Single-output safety checks miss escalation patterns that unfold over time.

The failure mode in AI safety isn't intent. It's timing.

What Ikwe's Work Shows

Risk signals appear early—before standard checks surface them.

Early signals are detectable at the trajectory level, not just in single outputs
Risk patterns repeat across cases, contexts, and deployment types
Standard checks miss them because they evaluate outputs, not trajectories
Earlier visibility changes decisions—increasing time to intervene before escalation

Ikwe doesn't predict outcomes. Ikwe changes when teams see risk—and timing changes outcomes.

How Ikwe Works

Three layers of safety infrastructure

Safety Audit & Benchmark

A structured evaluation of how your AI behaves in emotionally sensitive, high-risk, and human-impact contexts.

What you get
  • Risk classification by severity and likelihood
  • Intervention gaps and escalation failures
  • Board- and compliance-ready documentation

Compliance & Monitoring Layer

Continuous monitoring to ensure safety remains visible as systems scale and change.

What you get
  • Risk drift alerts across sessions
  • Trend dashboards and incident logs
  • Evidence of proactive governance

Crisis & Intervention Runtime

Real-time safety response when interactions cross predefined thresholds.

What you get
  • Escalation protocols and guardrails
  • Human review pathways
  • Audit trails for every intervention
