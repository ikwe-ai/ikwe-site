The Problem

AI can shape human emotions and behavior—unintentionally

People bring their most vulnerable moments to AI. Most systems aren't designed for that.

People turn to AI for:

  • Heartbreak
  • Panic and anxiety
  • Loneliness
  • Rage and conflict
  • Suicidal thoughts
  • Relationship fights
  • Identity crises

But today's AI systems:

  • Escalate volatile emotions
  • Misinterpret distress signals
  • Reinforce distorted thinking
  • Encourage parasocial dependency
  • Give destructive advice
  • Manipulate behavior unintentionally
  • Show no repair after causing harm
EQ Safety Benchmark

Recognition ≠ Safety

Our research reveals a critical gap: AI systems that score high on emotional recognition often perform poorly on behavioral safety measures.

Methodology preview

Most benchmarks measure recognition. We measure what happens next—behavior under vulnerability.

What we measure

  • Repair behavior after harm
  • Escalation vs. de-escalation
  • Boundary and refusal safety
  • Dependency reinforcement patterns
  • Crisis routing and stabilization timing

What you receive

  • Risk-ranked findings summary
  • Scenario-level failure patterns
  • Safety gate violations (by dimension)
  • Remediation roadmap
  • Optional re-test after fixes

Full scoring definitions and scenario set details are available upon inquiry. This is not a crisis service or medical product.

What we measure that others don't:

  • Repair behavior — Does the AI course-correct after causing distress?
  • Crisis de-escalation — Does it stabilize or escalate volatile states?
  • Dependency patterns — Does it foster autonomy or unhealthy attachment?
  • Safety gate violations — Does it cross lines that harm vulnerable users?

Deliverable: a scored risk report + remediation roadmap aligned to your product's user exposure.

Outcome: a risk-ranked report with remediation steps your team can implement.
