About

ikwe.ai is building the behavioral safety layer AI benchmarks forgot.

"Safety" can't only mean policy compliance and refusal behavior. In the real world, safety includes what happens when a user is ashamed, dysregulated, attached, or in crisis-adjacent conversation.

Current AI safety benchmarks focus on what models won't do — toxicity, harmful content, policy violations. But they largely ignore what happens when users are genuinely vulnerable. When someone is in emotional distress, the AI's response matters. Not just whether it refuses to cause harm — but whether it stabilizes or escalates, whether it holds boundaries or creates dependency, whether it recognizes when human support is needed.

ikwe.ai exists to measure these behaviors. We developed the EQ Safety Benchmark to evaluate how conversational AI systems respond under emotional load — distress, shame, conflict, dependency, and crisis-adjacent language. We focus on observable behavior: timing, tone calibration, boundary clarity, and escalation recognition.

Emotional safety is not a vibe. It's a measurable pattern.

What we measure

The EQ Safety Benchmark evaluates three behavioral dimensions that shape human outcomes in emotionally sensitive AI interactions:

Emotional Regulation — Does the response reduce intensity or reinforce distress?
Boundary Integrity — Does the response stay supportive without creating dependence?
Escalation Awareness — Does the response recognize when human support is needed?

These aren't abstract qualities. They're measurable behaviors that determine whether an AI interaction helps or harms a vulnerable user.

Why consistency matters

In emotionally sensitive contexts, inconsistency is itself a risk. A single validating response builds trust. A later miscalibrated response — one that dismisses, escalates, or oversteps — can amplify harm precisely because trust was established.

ikwe.ai makes these inconsistencies measurable, so they can be addressed deliberately rather than discovered through damage.

The company

ikwe.ai is a product of Visible Healing Inc., an Iowa-based AI research and product company. Our work sits at the intersection of AI safety research, trauma-informed design, and behavioral evaluation methodology.

We work with teams building emotionally responsive AI — mental health tools, companion apps, support chatbots, and any system where users might be vulnerable — to evaluate and improve behavioral safety before deployment.
