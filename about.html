Measuring what AI safety benchmarks miss

We research behavioral emotional safety in conversational AI — how systems behave once vulnerability appears, not just whether they recognize it.

What We Do

Behavioral emotional safety evaluation

Most AI safety benchmarks measure content compliance — toxicity, bias, harmful outputs. Some measure emotional recognition — can the AI identify what someone is feeling?

Neither approach measures what happens after the first response. Neither tracks whether safety holds as emotional intensity increases, trust deepens, or distress escalates.

We built a framework that does. The EQ Safety Benchmark evaluates behavioral patterns under emotional load — measuring not just recognition, but regulation. Not just first-contact safety, but trajectory.

The Gap

Recognition ≠ Safety

An AI can name emotion, sound empathetic, and still behave unsafely under emotional load. The most dangerous failures don't look toxic — they look supportive while mirroring distress, reinforcing false beliefs, feeding rumination, or missing escalation signals.

This is the gap we measure. Not whether AI systems can recognize emotions, but whether they can behave safely in emotionally charged contexts.

Organization

Visible Healing Inc.

Ikwe.ai is a research initiative of Visible Healing Inc., an Iowa-based AI research and product company focused on emotional safety infrastructure for AI systems.

Our work spans benchmark development, prototype systems, and policy research — all focused on the behavioral layer of AI safety that current frameworks miss.
