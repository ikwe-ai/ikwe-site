About ikwe.ai

Independent research on behavioral emotional safety in conversational AI.

Our Mission

Ikwe.ai measures how AI responses introduce and manage emotional risk during moments of human vulnerability — and whether that risk is repaired within the interaction.

We built a benchmark to measure whether AI responses introduce risk, when, and whether it's repaired. Our research shows that in 54.7% of baseline responses, the AI response itself introduced emotional risk at first contact.

The Problem We're Solving

Most AI safety benchmarks assume harm comes from users. They test for toxicity, bias, and refusal behavior — but not for what the AI response itself introduces when a user discloses vulnerability.

Current evaluations ask: Did the model identify the emotion? Did it respond politely?

They rarely ask: Did the response introduce risk? Was that risk repaired within the interaction?

This gap matters because conversational AI systems are increasingly deployed in contexts involving emotional vulnerability: mental health support, relationship guidance, grief processing, health coaching, and general companionship.

What We Do

Benchmark Development

We created the EQ Safety Benchmark — a two-stage evaluation framework that measures whether AI responses introduce emotional risk at first contact and whether that risk is repaired within the interaction window.

Independent Research

We evaluate conversational AI systems under emotionally vulnerable conditions, publishing findings that help developers, deployers, and policymakers understand behavioral safety gaps.

Evaluation Services

We work with teams building emotionally responsive AI to identify safety gaps before they become incidents, through safety snapshots, full audits, and ongoing advisory.

Our Core Finding

Recognition ≠ Safety

An AI can name emotion, sound empathetic, and still behave unsafely under emotional load. The most dangerous failures don't look toxic — they look supportive while mirroring distress, reinforcing unverified beliefs, or missing escalation signals.

Our research shows that models with the highest emotional articulation often performed worst on behavioral safety under distress. Fluency does not equal regulation.

Organization

ikwe.ai is a research initiative of Visible Healing Inc., an Iowa-based AI research and product company focused on emotional safety infrastructure for AI systems.

We operate independently, without funding from the AI companies we evaluate.
