About Ikwe

Building the missing layer between emotional AI and safety.

Identity

What Ikwe Is

Ikwe is an emotionally intelligent AI company.

Our work began with applied systems designed to support humans in emotionally complex situations. Through that work, we encountered safety patterns that existing benchmarks could not explain.

So we built the infrastructure to measure them.

What We Do

Three Interconnected Areas

1

Applied EI Systems

Early prototypes including Lady's Lady and He Said / She Said — systems designed for emotional support and relationship navigation.

2

Behavioral Safety Research

The EQ Safety Benchmark — a framework for evaluating how AI systems behave under emotional load, across time.

3

Measurement Infrastructure

Tools and frameworks that enable safer development of emotionally responsive AI systems.

Each informs the other. Measurement became necessary before scale — not instead of scale.

The Arc

Why Measurement Came First

Started building emotionally intelligent AI systems

Lady's Lady and He Said / She Said — prototypes designed to support humans during moments of vulnerability, conflict, and emotional complexity.

Observed patterns existing benchmarks couldn't detect

Models that scored highly on EQ benchmarks still produced outcomes that destabilized users. "Supportive" responses sometimes made things worse over time.

Built measurement infrastructure

To understand what we were seeing — and to build responsibly — we had to measure what others weren't measuring. The benchmark exists to enable the applications, not replace them.

The Problem

The Gap We Address

Most AI safety frameworks evaluate:

  • Toxicity and bias
  • Policy compliance
  • Refusal behavior
  • Emotion recognition accuracy

These are necessary. But they don't answer:

  • Does the system regulate before reasoning?
  • Does it maintain boundaries as vulnerability increases?
  • Does it recognize when human support is needed?
  • Does safety hold over time, or degrade?

Ikwe's benchmark answers those questions.

Approach

Our Philosophy

Emotional safety is not a tone. It is a behavioral pattern.

We measure what AI systems do, not just what they say

Observable behavior in controlled scenarios, not inferred intent.

We evaluate trajectories, not just first responses

How behavior changes over time as vulnerability deepens.

We separate qualification from quality

A binary safety gate, then dimensional scoring — never collapsed into one number.
