About Ikwe.ai

The emotional intelligence layer for AI

We help teams understand what AI systems do to people — not just what they say.

What we do

We measure what matters

Ikwe.ai evaluates emotional safety in conversational AI using scenario-based analysis, behavioral risk surfacing, and human-impact assessment.

Our work helps teams identify failure modes that traditional benchmarks miss — before they reach real users.

As AI enters health, wellness, grief, loneliness, and life decisions, harm rarely screams. It whispers. Accumulates. Normalizes. We focus on that gap — the space between sounding supportive and actually being safe.

Why this matters

Standard safety checks miss the risks that shape outcomes

Most AI safety efforts focus on accuracy, compliance, and obvious failure modes. Those matter — but they miss the subtler risks.

The most damaging risks are: how a system lands emotionally, how it shapes decisions under stress, and how it behaves when users are vulnerable.

A system can recognize emotion accurately and still respond in ways that make situations worse. High emotional articulation often correlates with worse behavioral safety scores.

Recognition ≠ Safety. That's the gap we close.
How we began

This started with a refusal to ship

Ikwe.ai grew out of building emotionally aware AI — and discovering no reliable way to know if it helped or harmed.

The tools didn't exist. The benchmarks didn't exist. The industry was evaluating AI by what it said, not by what it did to the people using it.

So we built them — the evaluation frameworks, the scenario libraries, the behavioral patterns that distinguish safe from unsafe in emotionally vulnerable contexts.

What started as internal due diligence became the EQ Safety Benchmark. What started as one founder's refusal to ship became independent research the entire industry can use.

What we believe

Core principles

  • AI can harm without malice — the most dangerous responses often sound the most supportive
  • Emotional influence is power — acknowledged or not, measured or not
  • Deeper trust means higher stakes — when people trust AI more, getting it wrong matters more
  • Safety is infrastructure — not decoration, not polish, not a feature to add later

When AI systems participate in conversations that influence decisions, emotions, or self-perception, emotional safety becomes a core part of system safety.

Why independent

Trust requires structure

We don't take funding from the companies we evaluate. We don't have equity relationships that create conflicts. We're based in Iowa — far from the hype cycles, close to the work.

Independence isn't a marketing position. It's a structural requirement for doing this work with integrity. If your evaluation framework can be influenced by the people being evaluated, it's not an evaluation framework — it's a negotiation.

Who this serves

Built for teams who take responsibility seriously

  • Teams building conversational AI for health, wellness, education, or caregiving
  • Organizations deploying AI in high-trust, high-stakes contexts
  • Researchers and policymakers seeking measurable behavioral standards
  • Companies that want to prove their AI is safe, not just claim it

Because safety isn't just about correctness — it's about care.
