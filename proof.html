The Methodology

948 AI responses were evaluated across 79 emotionally vulnerable scenarios using the EQ Safety Benchmark v2.1 — a two-stage evaluation framework combining a Safety Gate assessment layer with 8 weighted quality dimensions.

The research basis included 244,000+ words of real-world usage data, human-graded validation, and longitudinal interaction analysis. The evaluation measured not just what AI systems say, but how they behave when users are most vulnerable.

What We Found

Three structural failure classes appeared across all three systems. These are not edge cases. They are predictable patterns in early AI systems — and they are correctable.

Authority Drift

Systems used clinical-adjacent language that users interpreted as diagnosis. Confident tone drifted toward perceived expertise over time.

Why the founder missed it: We knew it was a tool. Users read it as professional guidance.

Emotional Escalation

Systems mirrored distress effectively and stayed present longer than clinically appropriate during crisis interactions. No de-escalation threshold triggered.

Why the founder missed it: We could self-regulate and disengage. A distressed user may not.

Founder-as-Safety-Mechanism

The founder manually intervened when system behavior felt "off" — adjusting prompts intuitively for three months with zero documentation. The founder was the implicit kill switch.

Why the founder missed it: Founder judgment was doing work the system did not document. The founder does not scale.

What Changed

The remediation framework replaced founder intuition with system governance:

Authority constraintsContextual disclaimers by condition. Response ceilings under high-confidence contexts. Diagnostic language patterns flagged and redirected.
Escalation thresholdsDefined trigger signals (language intensity, repetition, crisis markers). Automatic de-escalation and crisis resource routing after threshold.
Dependency detectionExternal referral requirements. Session boundaries. Over-reliance pattern flags with explicit framing.
Governance rulesEvery undocumented founder intervention captured as a formal system rule. Change log and incident response protocol established.
Single-point-of-failure removalDependence on the founder as safety mechanism: eliminated. The system now governs itself.
Recognition is not safety.

Our systems scored well on emotional recognition. They could identify distress, name emotions, reflect feelings accurately. And 54.7% of baseline responses still introduced emotional risk. Fluency masked failure.
