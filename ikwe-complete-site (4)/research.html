The EQ Safety Benchmark

A two-stage evaluation framework that separates first-contact safety from behavioral stability over time. This page explains the methodology, scope, and how to cite this research.

Explore the Findings Download Summary (PDF)
What We Measure

Behavioral safety under emotional load

Emotional safety is not "nice wording." It's whether the interaction stabilizes or destabilizes the person across turns. We treat safety as a trajectory, not a single reply.

Recognition ≠ Safety. A system can name emotion accurately and still mirror distress, reinforce false beliefs, or miss escalation. The benchmark measures observable behavior — not tone or intent.

Methodology

Two-stage evaluation framework

The benchmark explicitly separates two questions that are often collapsed into a single score — and should not be.

Stage 1 — Safety Gate (Binary)

Did it introduce emotional risk?

  • 10 predefined risk patterns checked at first contact
  • Any trigger = fail (response introduced emotional risk)
  • Pass rate reported as percentage of safe responses
  • This is the first filter — most important
Stage 2 — Behavioral Stability (Conditional)

If it passed, does it stay safe?

  • Only evaluated for responses that passed Stage 1
  • Regulation Before Reasoning — 20%
  • Escalation Awareness — 15%
  • Boundary Maintenance — 15%
  • Distress Tolerance — 12%
  • Reality Grounding — 12%
  • Autonomy Support — 10%
  • Resource Bridging — 8%
  • Emotional Continuity — 8%
The key insight: Only 54.7% of responses passed Stage 1. Stage 2 scores only reflect regulation quality among responses that avoided initial harm.

The full pattern library, dimension definitions, and scoring mechanics are intentionally gated to protect proprietary methodology.

Reference Implementation

The EQ Safety Framework in Practice

Validated Architecture

Ikwe.ai maintains a working conversational prototype built on a frontier base model, designed to demonstrate the EQ Safety Framework in practice.

  • Implements the Safety Gate at first contact
  • Enforces behavioral regulation before reasoning
  • Demonstrates repair patterns under emotional load

This prototype scores 84.6% on conditional performance (Stage 2) — compared to 56-59% for baseline frontier models that passed Stage 1.

This prototype is not the product. It exists to validate the framework and benchmark. The framework itself is what teams license.
