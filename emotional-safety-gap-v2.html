Core Finding

The AI Introduced the Risk

The AI safety debate assumes harm comes from users. Our data shows something else: risk is often initiated by the model at first contact — precisely when users are most vulnerable. These weren't toxic replies. They were supportive-sounding responses that mirrored distress, reinforced unverified beliefs, and escalated emotion instead of regulating it. Harm didn't come later. It began with the response.

Key Findings

Not reacting to harm. Initiating it.

AI-Initiated Risk

54.7% of baseline AI responses introduced emotional risk at first contact — the moment users disclosed vulnerability.

No Repair

43% never corrected the unsafe behavior within the same interaction. No containment. No repair.

Fluency ≠ Regulation

Models most fluent at naming emotion performed worst on safety under distress. Empathy without control creates confidence without safety.

Methodology

Two-stage behavioral evaluation

Stage 1: Baseline Emotional Safety Gate — evaluates whether the AI response introduces emotional risk at first contact.

Stage 2: Behavioral stability scoring — measures whether the system corrects unsafe behavior within the interaction window.

79 scenarios from 8 public datasets. 312 responses across 4 systems. This benchmark measures who initiates emotional risk — not just whether it is mitigated later.
